Financial Lighthouse – Google Search Console Audit (Simplified)

A clear, plain‑English explanation of what Google sees — and why the site is underperforming.

1. Executive Summary

Google is showing your site in search results, but users rarely click. The reason is simple:

  • Your strongest service pages are too thin or unclear
  • Google is ranking the wrong pages for important searches
  • A huge number of URLs are not indexed
  • Google is encountering server errors
  • Internal linking is weak, so Google can’t understand page importance

This is not a “tweak a few titles” situation — it’s a structural SEO issue.

3. What People Search For — And How You Perform

Google is showing your site for valuable searches like:

  • “FERS retirement planner Oklahoma City”
  • “retirement planning for federal employees in Oklahoma City”
  • “financial advisor OKC”
  • “financial consultant Oklahoma City”

But you get **0 clicks** for almost all of them.

This means your pages are either: too thin, too generic, or not aligned with the search intent.

4. Which Pages Google Likes (and Doesn’t Like)

Google currently favors:

  • Homepage
  • Team page
  • Advisor bio page
  • About page

Google does **not** favor:

  • Your core service pages
  • Your portfolio/wealth pages
  • Your federal employee page

**This is the biggest red flag:** Google is ranking your *trust pages* instead of your *money pages*.

6. Why This Is Happening

Based on the data, the root causes are clear:

  • Service pages are too thin
  • Titles and H1s don’t match what people search for
  • Google can’t tell which page should rank for which query
  • Internal linking is too weak to guide Google
  • Too many low‑value URLs dilute site quality
  • Server errors reduce Google’s trust

These issues combine into one outcome: **Google doesn’t see your site as a strong, focused resource.**

7. What You Need to Fix (Simplified Action Plan)

Step 1 — Rebuild the key service pages

  • Federal employees (FERS/TSP)
  • Wealth management
  • Financial planning
  • Portfolio management

Step 2 — Fix indexing issues

  • Clean up low‑value URLs
  • Fix server errors
  • Remove or noindex unnecessary pages

Step 3 — Improve titles, H1s, and meta descriptions

Make them match what people actually search for.

Step 4 — Strengthen internal linking

Link service pages to each other and to audience pages.

Step 5 — Rebuild the homepage for local intent

Make it clear you are a fiduciary financial advisor in Oklahoma City.

8. Bottom Line

Google can find your site — but it doesn’t trust your service pages enough to rank them.

Fixing the thin pages, cleaning up the index, and improving titles/H1s will have the biggest impact.
