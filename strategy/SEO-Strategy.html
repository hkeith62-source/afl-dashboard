Financial Lighthouse

SEO Strategy & Execution Plan

Prepared from uploaded audit reports, Search Console exports, competitive analysis, content map, and keyword scorecard

April 2026

Bottom line

The site already shows up in Google, but it is not strong enough to win clicks, attract the right clients, or take advantage of its niche strengths. The plan is to:

  • Clean up technical issues so Google can trust and index the site properly.
  • Rebuild the pages that should bring in revenue.
  • Create focused pages for the audiences competitors are ignoring.

1. Executive diagnosis

  • The site appears in search results, but very few people click. Google is testing the site, but users often choose other results.
  • Bio and trust pages perform better than core service pages, which means the main service pages are not strong or specific enough.
  • A large number of excluded URLs and server errors (5xx) make it harder for Google to crawl and index the site.
  • Local SEO is weak: the Google Business Profile is incomplete, reviews are low, and local visibility is limited.
  • The biggest growth opportunity is niche audiences: women in transition, business owners, couples/families, inheritance situations, federal employees, and African American clients.

2. Evidence-backed conclusions

  • This is not just a visibility issue – it’s a competitiveness issue.
  • The federal/FERS niche already shows strong search impressions, but the current page is too weak to earn clicks.
  • The site has more potential than its current structure allows. The content map shows a realistic 13-week plan to expand into low-competition topics.
  • Mobile performance is slow, creating a poor first impression in a trust-sensitive industry.
  • Local competitors are generic. Financial Lighthouse can win by combining fiduciary positioning with audience-specific pages and selective city pages.

5. Strategy pillars

Pillar 1 - Fix crawl, indexation, and technical drag

  • Export all excluded URLs and decide whether to keep, improve, redirect, noindex, canonicalize, or remove them.
  • Fix all 5xx errors and confirm in Search Console.
  • Stop changing URLs until redirect rules and a master URL list are in place.
  • Reduce low-value or duplicate pages so Google focuses on the pages that matter.

Pillar 2 - Rebuild the pages that should drive revenue

  • Rewrite the homepage and main service pages with clear audiences, process explanations, FAQs, and calls to action.
  • Rebuild the federal employee page first – demand is proven, but the current page is failing.
  • Add crawlable text to investment/portfolio pages where important content is currently locked inside graphics.

Pillar 3 - Own the niche clusters competitors ignore

  • Follow the content map: women, widows, business owners, retirement plans, couples/families, then city pages.
  • Keep each page focused on one main intent.
  • Support service pages with educational content like fiduciary comparisons, advisor-selection guides, and inheritance topics.

Pillar 4 - Strengthen local prominence and conversion trust

  • Complete the Google Business Profile with original photos, full services, and regular posts.
  • Start a review-request program and expand local/industry citations.
  • Create at least one downloadable guide tied to a niche page to convert visitors into leads.

8. Final recommendation

  • Do not rely on generic advisor SEO. Lead with niche audience pages, stronger service pages, and selective city pages.
  • Treat the federal employee page as the first major win – impressions already prove demand.
  • Use the implementation tracker to manage sequencing and ownership across technical, content, local SEO, and reporting work.
  • After the first 90 days, shift from fixing to compounding: publish 1–2 targeted pieces per month, keep reviews coming in, and refresh weaker pages quarterly.
